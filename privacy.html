Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

Summary

NAVINCLUD respects your privacy. This extension was designed with privacy by default — no personal data is collected, stored on servers, or shared with third parties.

Data Collected

The extension collects and stores exclusively on the user's device:

  • • Ishihara test results — plates viewed, response times, score and diagnosis. Used only to display the result to the user.
  • • Filter preferences — selected filter type, intensity and shift adjustment. Used to apply the personalized visual correction.
  • • Unique test identifier — randomly generated UUID to organize the local test history. Does not allow user identification.

Storage

  • • All data is stored exclusively in chrome.storage.local — an internal browser storage area, isolated and inaccessible to other sites or extensions.
  • • No data is transmitted to remote servers, external APIs, or third parties.
  • • Data stays on the device until the user chooses to export or remove it.

Data We Do NOT Collect

  • ✖ Personal identifiable information — name, email, ID numbers, address
  • ✖ Browsing data — history, URLs visited (except when the filter is active to apply visual correction)
  • ✖ Credentials — passwords, tokens, authentication cookies
  • ✖ Analytics or trackers — the extension does not use Google Analytics, telemetry or any monitoring service

Third-Party Sharing

NAVINCLUD does not share any data with third parties. There is no integration with external APIs, advertising services, or social networks.

Your Rights (LGPD & GDPR)

In compliance with the Brazilian General Data Protection Law (LGPD — Law 13.709/2018) and GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Confirmation and access — view your test data in the extension popup
  • Correction — take a new test to update your results
  • Deletion — export data and clear local storage via the popup
  • Portability — export your tests in JSON format
  • Transparency — this policy details exactly how data is handled

Security

The extension operates with minimum necessary permissions and all processing is done locally. Local storage access is isolated by the browser itself, following the Manifest V3 security model.

Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated to reflect changes in the extension or legal requirements. The last update date is at the top of the page.
